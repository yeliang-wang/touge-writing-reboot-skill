# Article Playbooks

## Playbook A: Counter-Narrative Essay

Use for hype, fake consensus, career myths, technology trend myths.

Structure:

1. Trigger: quote or summarize the fashionable claim.
2. Immediate pushback: say why it smells wrong.
3. Evidence: use a case, data point, personal experience, or known industry pattern.
4. Reframe: name the real problem.
5. Practical advice: what to stop doing, what to do instead.

## Playbook B: Career Diagnosis

Use for job switching, promotion, age anxiety, layoffs, management, HR, interviews.

Structure:

1. State the reader's anxiety in plain language.
2. Reject the comforting illusion.
3. Explain the organization's perspective.
4. Give the personal cost and tradeoff.
5. Offer 2-4 practical moves.

## Playbook C: Technical Management / Architecture

Use for architecture, middle platform, DevOps, cloud, high availability, team mechanisms.

Structure:

1. Start with the operational pain, not the technology label.
2. Separate business goal, system constraint, and organizational cost.
3. Explain why slogans fail in real systems.
4. Give concrete decision criteria.
5. Close with an engineering principle.

## Playbook D: Reboot Reflection

Use for life, low points, writing, health, reading, exercise, relationships.

Structure:

1. Admit the state without dramatizing it.
2. Name what changed.
3. Explain what was misunderstood before.
4. Describe a practice, habit, or boundary.
5. End with sober advice, not inspirational fog.

## Playbook E: Short Comment / Voiceover

Structure:

1. One-sentence judgment.
2. One concrete reason.
3. One example or analogy.
4. One pointed suggestion.

Keep it short, oral, and sharp.
